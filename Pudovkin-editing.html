Pudovkin's Five Principles of Editing

A foundational theory for building cinematic meaning

1. Contrast

Juxtapose opposing scenes or ideas to highlight their differences and create drama or irony.

2. Parallelism

Show similar actions or motifs in different locations/times. Suggests connections or themes.

3. Symbolism

Edit to associate an object or action with deeper meaning (e.g., cutting from a caged bird to a trapped person).

4. Simultaneity

Cross-cut scenes happening at the same time to build suspense or show how actions affect each other.

5. Leitmotiv

Repeat a shot, sound, or motif for emphasis and to build a pattern or mood (e.g., recurring shots of clocks).

Why care about these principles?

Pudovkin believed editing was the art of connecting ideas, not just shots. Use these tools to create meaning and emotion!
