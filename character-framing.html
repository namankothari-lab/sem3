Essential Camera Framing Techniques

A quick interactive guide for filmmakers & storytellers

Extreme Close-Up (ECU)

Shows a detail (like an eye or hand). Used for intensity or to focus the viewer's attention on something critical.

Close-Up (CU)

Medium Close-Up (MCU)

Frames subject from chest up. Balances emotion and body language; common in interviews and dialogue scenes.

Medium Shot (MS)

Frames subject from waist up. Shows body language and some environment. Most common shot in film and TV.

Medium Long Shot (MLS)

Frames subject from knees up. Shows more of the setting, often used for group shots or movement.

Long Shot (LS) / Wide Shot

Shows full body and environment. Used for action, establishing space, or showing relationships.

Extreme Long Shot (ELS)

Shows landscape or cityscape. Establishes setting, scale, or mood. Used at beginnings or transitions.

How to choose the right framing?

Think about the emotion, information, and point of view you want to convey.
